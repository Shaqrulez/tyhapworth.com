Ty Hapworth

Salem's City Councillor At-Large since 2020

Meet Ty Join the Newsletter

As Salem's longest serving City Councillor, Ty Hapworth has served Salem for years, working tirelessly to expand affordable housing, invest in our parks, protect historic neighborhoods, and stand up for every resident. Veteran leadership for Salem.

Meet Ty

I grew up in rural Maine, where my dad was a teacher and my mom worked in healthcare. They taught me the values of hard work and service. After 9/11, I joined the Army and served as a combat arms officer, where I learned grit, accountability, and that leadership means putting others first.

I met my wife Micah in that same small town. We chose Salem to build our life, raise our kids, and put down roots. That was a choice, and it's a commitment I take seriously every day I serve on this Council.

Since winning a seat in 2019, I've served as Council President, chaired key committees, and worked to deliver real progress. I bring to City Hall the same approach I learned in the Army and apply every day in my career: listen, stay accountable, and get things done.

Who I Am

Values

01
Bravery

I believe in following my moral compass, and doing what is right, despite the personal or professional implications. I believe in having the courage to admit that I can learn from others. Our family motto is "Be Brave. No Excuses."

02
Authenticity

I say what I mean, and ask for what I think is right. As a mentor to young leaders, I've always encouraged them to be honest and transparent in their interactions.

03

I treat others with respect and seek to understand their perspective and point of view regardless of my own opinion. I love a political debate, but always give others the benefit of the doubt.

What I Fight For

Policy Priorities

🏛️
Good Government

Local government should be practical, disciplined, and focused on results. Its purpose is to solve real problems, protect vulnerable residents, and improve daily life. When national issues affect people locally, it should respond clearly and concretely. But its highest calling is to show that government closest to the people can still work with seriousness, competence, and purpose.

🏠
Affordable Housing

Salem's housing crisis demands bold action, smarter zoning, inclusionary requirements, accessory dwelling units, and real protections for renters and seniors.

🌿
Climate Action

From street trees and green infrastructure to clean energy leadership and bike connectivity, Salem must protect its coastline and lead as a resilient city.

🏛️
Historic Preservation

Our historic neighborhoods give Salem its identity and pride. Development must honor what makes this city irreplaceable while building for the next generation.

🏳️‍🌈
LGBTQ+ Rights

Every Salem resident deserves to live, work, and raise a family free from discrimination. I've stood up for our LGBTQ+ neighbors and always will.

🤝
Community and Equity

A city that works for everyone means investing in public spaces, engaging underserved neighborhoods, and ensuring every voice is heard at the table.

🫡
Service

Service means remembering that this office is not about us, it is about Salem. We should lead with humility, take nothing personally, and make every decision based on what best serves this city and the people who call it home.

The Record

What We've
Done Together

🏠

Accessory Dwelling Units

Made it legal and easier for homeowners to build in-law apartments and accessory homes, helping seniors, families, and renters find affordable options.

⚖️

Affordable Housing Requirements

Required affordable housing in large new developments, so working families aren't priced out of Salem.

🌳

$16 Million in Park Improvements

Bonded for $16 million in park improvements, investing in playgrounds, ballfields, and the green spaces that bring our community together.

⚡

Offshore Wind Port

Supported offshore wind marshalling port in Salem, creating clean energy jobs and making our city a climate leader.

🏛️

Demolition Delay Ordinance

Protected historic homes and neighborhoods with a demolition delay ordinance, making sure history isn't erased overnight.

🏘️

Renter Protections

Passed new protections for renters in condo conversions, giving tenants more time, notice, and stability when apartments turn into condos.

Proud to serve Salem. Every day.
